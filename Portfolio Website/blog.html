What I Learned from Building My First Website

Posted on July 5, 2025

When I built my first website using only HTML, I realized how important structure and semantics are. I used tags like <header>, <nav>, and <footer> to give it meaning.

"Don't chase perfection in the beginning — chase completion."

I struggled a bit with nesting and closing tags correctly, but that made me appreciate the foundation of every webpage. Consistency and clarity made my website feel professional, even though it was very basic.

Why HTML Is the Best Place to Start

Posted on July 8, 2025

Many beginners rush into JavaScript or frameworks, but I believe learning HTML first gives you a solid foundation. It's like learning the grammar of a language before writing poetry.

Once I understood tags, nesting, and semantic structure, I could build any layout I imagined — without being distracted by styling. If you're new to web development, start with HTML. It builds your confidence and gives immediate results.
